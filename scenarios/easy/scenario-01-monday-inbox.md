# Scenario 01: Executive Inbox — Monday Morning

## Executive Context
You support Madam Alicia, a busy executive who prefers short, polished, and direct emails.

## Time Limit
20 minutes

## Fake Inbox

### Email 1
**From:** Sarah Miller, Client  
**Subject:** Urgent: Need to Reschedule Today’s Meeting

Hi, something came up on our side and we need to reschedule today’s 2:00 PM meeting. Can Madam Alicia do later today or tomorrow morning?

### Email 2
**From:** Internal Operations Team  
**Subject:** Weekly Status Update

Sharing the weekly operations status report for visibility. No action needed.

### Email 3
**From:** Vendor Billing  
**Subject:** Invoice Approval Needed

Attached is the invoice for this month’s services. Please confirm approval so we can process payment.

### Email 4
**From:** John Reyes, Client  
**Subject:** Follow-up on Pending Request

Just following up on the document I requested last week. Please advise when this can be sent.

### Email 5
**From:** Calendar Notification  
**Subject:** Conflict: Monday Leadership Meeting

There is a scheduling conflict between the Leadership Meeting and the Client Strategy Call.

## Learner Tasks
1. Prioritize each email.
2. Draft replies for Email 1 and Email 4.
3. Flag Email 3 and Email 5 for escalation.
4. Write a short final summary to the executive.
